Discovering Tunisia

Haajar's First Visit — Between Tunis & Beni Khiar — Jul 27 to Aug 10

🏫
Medina of Tunis
A UNESCO World Heritage labyrinth of ancient souks, hidden courtyards, and the grand Zitouna Mosque. Lose yourself in the narrow alleys lined with spice vendors, copper artisans, and traditional perfume shops.
Culture History
💙
Sidi Bou Said
The most photographed village in Tunisia. Whitewashed walls, cobalt-blue doors, and bougainvillea everywhere. Sip mint tea at the legendary Café des Nattes while overlooking the Gulf of Tunis.
Culture
🏛️
Carthage Ruins
Walk through Punic and Roman ruins that once rivalled Rome itself. Climb Byrsa Hill for sweeping views, explore the massive Antonine Baths, and stand where Hannibal once ruled. UNESCO World Heritage.
History
🎨
Bardo Museum
Home to the world's finest collection of Roman mosaics, displayed in a former Ottoman palace. Stunning floors and walls covered in intricate ancient art spanning centuries of North African civilisation.
History Culture
🏖️
Beni Khiar
Your home base on the Cap Bon coast. Known for traditional Tunisian foutas (hand-woven textiles), local beach access, and an authentic small-town pace. The perfect launchpad for exploring the peninsula.
Beach Culture
🌊
Hammamet
Just next door to Beni Khiar, this resort town has golden beaches, an old medina with jasmine-scented gardens, and a lively waterfront. Perfect for lazy beach days and sunset strolls.
Beach
🍣
Nabeul
The ceramics capital of Tunisia, right next to Beni Khiar. Hit the famous Friday market for hand-painted pottery, colourful tiles, and local produce. A feast for the senses and perfect for picking up souvenirs.
Culture Adventure
🌝
La Marsa Beach
A trendy beach suburb of Tunis with a relaxed vibe, waterfront cafés, and golden sand. Stroll the corniche at sunset, grab street food, and watch the city skyline glow against the Mediterranean.
Beach
🍴
Tunisian Food Guide
Must-try dishes: brik (crispy egg pastry), lablebi (chickpea soup), couscous with lamb, ojja (spicy egg and tomato shakshuka-style), bambalouni (Tunisian doughnuts), and endless glasses of sweet mint tea.
Food
🌃
Cap Bon Peninsula
The dramatic peninsula curving northeast from Tunis. Take coastal drives past vineyards and citrus groves, visit the ancient hot springs at Korbous, and find secluded beaches along the rugged shore.
Adventure
🏛️
El Jem Amphitheatre
If you take a day trip south, this is the third-largest Roman amphitheatre in the world, remarkably preserved. Walk the underground chambers where gladiators once waited. UNESCO World Heritage and absolutely jaw-dropping.
History Adventure
🏢
Dar Essid & Traditional Dars
Step inside a centuries-old Tunisian townhouse. Ornate tilework, carved stucco, interior courtyards with fountains. These historic homes offer a window into traditional Tunisian family life and architecture.
Culture History
💡 Tips for Haajar
  • Arabic is the main language — Tunisian dialect (Derja) is close to Libyan Arabic, so you will understand most of it
  • Currency is the Tunisian Dinar (TND). ATMs are widely available; carry small notes for souks
  • Summer is HOT — expect 30–40°C. Stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, plan activities for mornings and evenings
  • Modest dress is appreciated in medinas and mosques — a light scarf is useful
  • Haggling is expected in souks — start at half the asking price and enjoy the back-and-forth
  • Try local fruit — summer means figs, peaches, watermelon, and prickly pear everywhere
